Hotels by Island

Each Hawaiian island feels different. So your hotel choice should match the trip you're really planning. Here's how the islands break down for travelers.

Oahu

The Gathering Place

Home to Honolulu, Waikiki, and the iconic North Shore. So this is where most travelers begin Hawaii.

  • The Royal Hawaiian (Waikiki)
  • Halekulani (Waikiki)
  • Turtle Bay Resort (North Shore)
  • Aulani Disney Resort (Ko Olina)
  • Kahala Hotel & Resort

Maui

The Valley Isle

Home to the Road to Hana, Haleakala, and Hawaii's deepest concentration of beach resorts.

  • Four Seasons Resort Maui at Wailea
  • Grand Wailea
  • Montage Kapalua Bay
  • The Ritz-Carlton Maui, Kapalua
  • Andaz Maui at Wailea

Kauai

The Garden Isle

Older, greener, quieter. Therefore, this is where to go for waterfalls, cliffs, and slower mornings.

  • 1 Hotel Hanalei Bay (Princeville)
  • Grand Hyatt Kauai (Poipu)
  • Ko'a Kea Hotel & Resort (Poipu)
  • Timbers Kauai (Lihue)
  • Sheraton Kauai Resort (Poipu)

Big Island

Hawaii Island

The youngest and largest island. Active volcanoes, black-sand beaches, and stargazing on Mauna Kea.

  • Four Seasons Resort Hualalai
  • Mauna Lani, Auberge Resorts
  • The Westin Hapuna Beach Resort
  • Mauna Kea Beach Hotel
  • Fairmont Orchid Hawaii

Lanai

The Pineapple Isle

Just over 3,000 residents. Two famous Four Seasons resorts. Almost no traffic. Pure escape.

  • Four Seasons Resort Lanai
  • Sensei Lanai, A Four Seasons Resort
  • Hotel Lanai (Lanai City)

Molokai

The Friendly Isle

The most untouched Hawaiian island. No traffic lights. No resorts taller than a coconut tree. Pure Hawaii.

  • Hotel Molokai (Kaunakakai)
  • Vacation rentals dominate here
  • Ideal for travelers seeking authentic local culture

✓

~ Smart Travel ~

Hawaii Travel Tips You Should Know

A few small decisions can save you hundreds. So here's what experienced Hawaii travelers actually do.

1

Book Inter-Island Flights Early

Inter-island fares climb quickly. Therefore, lock in Hawaiian or Southwest as soon as your dates are set.

2

Rent a Car on Most Islands

Skip the rental on Oahu if you stay in Waikiki. However, you'll need one everywhere else.

3

Pack Reef-Safe Sunscreen

Hawaii law bans most chemical sunscreens. So bring mineral-only (zinc or titanium) products.

4

Shoulder Season Saves Money

Late April and early September deliver great weather. Plus rates drop noticeably. Therefore, plan around them.

5

Make Reservations for Popular Spots

Hanauma Bay, Diamond Head, and Haleakala sunrise all require advance bookings. So plan day one.

6

Tip Generously, Locally

Hawaii service workers depend on tips. So budget the standard 18–22%. Always carry cash for valets.

Hawaii Hotel FAQ

Real questions from real travelers. So here are direct answers, no fluff.

What's the best island in Hawaii for first-time visitors?
Oahu and Maui share the title. Oahu suits travelers who want urban energy plus beaches, world-class food, and easy access from the mainland. Maui suits travelers who want resort calm with day-trip adventure. Therefore, most first-timers split their trip between the two.
Which Hawaiian island has the best hotels?
Maui leads in luxury resort depth, particularly in Wailea and Kapalua. The Big Island holds the title for boutique wellness, especially Four Seasons Hualalai and Mauna Lani. Oahu wins for iconic, historic hotels like The Royal Hawaiian and Halekulani.
When is the cheapest time to visit Hawaii?
April (after Easter), early May, September, and early October offer the lowest rates and lighter crowds. Furthermore, the weather stays excellent across all islands during these months. So shoulder season is genuinely the best deal.
How much do Hawaii hotels typically cost?
Mid-range resorts start around $300–$500 per night. Luxury beachfront properties run $800–$2,500+ per night. Boutique and condo-style stays often deliver better value. However, resort fees, parking, and taxes can add 20–30% to the published rate.
What's the difference between Wailea and Waikiki?
Waikiki sits inside Honolulu — urban, walkable, and packed with restaurants, shops, and nightlife. Wailea, on Maui, is a quieter master-planned resort zone with calm beaches and luxury resorts. Therefore, Waikiki suits energy-seekers. Wailea suits relaxation-seekers.
Do Hawaii hotels charge resort fees?
Most do. Resort fees typically run $35–$60 per night. Notably, Four Seasons properties in Hawaii do not charge resort fees. So always confirm the total before you book.
Which Hawaii hotels are best for families?
Grand Wailea (Maui), Aulani Disney Resort (Oahu), Four Seasons Resort Maui at Wailea, and Hilton Hawaiian Village (Oahu) all run strong kids programs, multiple pools, and family-friendly suites. Furthermore, all sit on swimmable beaches.
Which Hawaii hotels are best for honeymoons?
Halekulani (Oahu), Hotel Wailea (adults-only, Maui), Montage Kapalua Bay (Maui), Four Seasons Resort Hualalai (Big Island), and Sensei Lanai stand out. All offer privacy, exceptional service, and sunset-friendly settings.
Should I rent a car in Hawaii?
Skip the car on Oahu if you base in Waikiki. However, rent on Maui, Kauai, and the Big Island — public transit is limited and the best beaches require a drive. Book early; rentals sell out, especially in peak season.
Are all-inclusive resorts a thing in Hawaii?
Not really. Hawaii has very few traditional all-inclusive resorts. Travelers generally pay separately for meals, activities, and excursions. However, some properties bundle breakfast or daily credit packages — ask before booking.
What's the best Hawaii hotel for snorkeling right from the beach?
Four Seasons Resort Lanai (Hulopoe Bay), Montage Kapalua Bay (Honolua-adjacent), and Four Seasons Hualalai (King's Pond) deliver excellent snorkeling steps from your room. Hawaii's marine life genuinely shines at these properties.
Is Hawaii safe for solo travelers?
Generally yes. Hawaii ranks among the safest US destinations. Standard precautions apply — lock the car, avoid isolated trails at night, and respect local customs. Solo female travelers report very positive experiences across all islands.
How many days do I need in Hawaii?
Seven nights minimum for one island. Ten to fourteen nights if you island-hop. So account for one travel day per island change. A rushed Hawaii trip tends to feel exhausting, not relaxing.
What is the rainiest island in Hawaii?
Kauai gets the most annual rainfall — that's why it's so green. However, rain typically falls in short bursts and clears quickly. Therefore, pack a light rain shell year-round and don't let weather forecasts scare you off.
Which Hawaii hotel has the best view?
Picks vary, but 1 Hotel Hanalei Bay (Kauai cliffs), Mauna Kea Beach Hotel (Big Island crescent beach), and Halekulani's Diamond Head suites consistently top the lists. So request a high-floor ocean-view room when booking.
What's the difference between Big Island and Hawaii Island?
They're the same place. "Big Island" is the local nickname for the island officially named Hawaii. The nickname avoids confusion with the entire state of Hawaii.
Can I see lava at Hawaii hotels?
Active lava only flows on the Big Island and only during eruption cycles. So no hotel "guarantees" lava views. Stay on the Kohala or Kona Coast and drive to Hawaii Volcanoes National Park for the experience.
Do Hawaii hotels offer airport shuttles?
Most do not. A few high-end resorts arrange transfers for an extra fee. So plan on Uber, Lyft, taxi, or rental car pickup at the airport. Confirm with your hotel ahead of time.
What's the dress code for Hawaii hotels and dining?
Almost everywhere is casual to "resort casual." Even at upscale restaurants, an aloha shirt and slacks work fine for men. Sundresses or skirts work for women. So pack light layers and one nicer outfit per traveler.
